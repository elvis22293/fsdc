Abstract

Rossio Tunnel is a centenary railway tunnel built between 1887 and 1890 in Lisbon, Portugal, which interconnects the Rossio and Campolide Train Stations. At its time, the construction of the Tunnel and the Rossio Station was considered the biggest Portuguese Engineering achievement of the XIX century. For safety reasons, in 2004 the owner of the Portuguese railway infrastructure, REFER, has decided to proceed with a major rehabilitation of the tunnel. Given the importance of this structure, it was also decided to install an automated large-scale convergence monitoring system throughout the entire length of the tunnel. Several solutions were evaluated based on different market-available technologies. Taking into account both technical and cost factors, a final decision was taken to install an all-fibre optic based sensing system to continuously monitor the structural behaviour of the tunnel during its life service. This decision goes in-line with the REFER strategy to automate as much as possible the structural health monitoring of its most relevant infrastructures.
The Rossio fibre optic structural health monitoring system was developed and installed by the consortium FiberSensing/EPOS, accordingly to REFER specifications. An extensiometric method (MEMCOT - Extensometric Method for Monitoring Convergences in Tunnels), developed by CEGEO-IST, was used for determining the tunnel convergence. The method uses strain measurements along a tunnel section contour to determine convergences between predefined positions. In Rossio Tunnel, eight fibre Bragg grating (FBG) sensors were used along the contour of each instrumented section â seven for strain measurement and one for temperature measurement and compensation â. This approach provides a monitoring system that is totally immune to EM/RF interferences and does not require the installation of any active equipment or component inside the entire length of the tunnel (2.6 km). In addition, it has only a marginal impact on the clearance gauge (less than 5 cm reduction).
A total of eight hundred and seventy two fibre Bragg grating sensors were used to instrument one hundred and nine sections of the tunnel. This was done by applying to each section wall a stainless steel bar previously instrumented with a single optical fibre containing a series of seven weldable strain sensors and one weldable temperature sensor. More than 12 km of optical fibre cables were installed to connect all the sensors to the measurement system, which consists on a FS5100 BraggMETER combined with a FS3300 USB Expansion Multiplexer 1Ã128. This system is installed at the entrance of the Tunnel and is remotely connected to a Data Management Software running on a Server at the REFER Data Centre, which receives, processes and storages all the data acquired by the measuring system.
Through a Web Interface, authorized users are able to monitor using graphs and tables all the relevant structural data (strain, temperature, convergence, velocity and acceleration of strain and convergence) in different ways (time evolution, comparison between sensors, sections and alignments), as well as define several types of SMS/email structural level alarms (system alarms are automatically generated by the measurement system). The acquisition rate can also be changed from continuous to one per day.
In this paper, the fibre optic solution developed for continuously monitoring convergence and other important structural parameters in Rossio Tunnel is described in detail, as well as all the steps involved in its development, implementation and installation during the final phase of the rehabilitation process in 2007 and 2008. The overall rehabilitation works are also briefly described. Particular emphasis is put on explaining the motivation of the structure owner to install an automatic structural health monitoring system, the methodology that was followed to select the sections to be instrumented, and the premises that led to the definition of the systemâs functional specifications. The operational results obtained with the monitoring system to this date are also analysed.
